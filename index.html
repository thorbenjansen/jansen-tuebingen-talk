Current work · PISA Foreign Language Assessment

PISA gives a controlled window into AI-supported writing

  • 1,699 students from Germany, age 15.
  • Two texts, 30 minutes each: one without AI support and one with AI support.
  • Process data makes AI-supported writing inspectable.

PISA writing study

Texts written with chatbots receive higher scores

Higher score distributions Across content, language quality, holistic quality, and organization, chatbot-supported texts shift toward higher score ranges.
Population sample The distributions compare texts written with and without chatbot support in the pre-sample.

Automated scoring during writing

Writing products can be scored reliably before students finish

Early signal Model quality rises quickly as more writing time becomes available.
Fusion helps most early The combined model performs best at short writing-time thresholds and remains strong at the full-text endpoint.

Writing process from keyboard data

Texts are stronger when students type more

Keylogging makes the writing process visible, not only the final text.

Using generative-AI text in writing

Texts are stronger when students paste in more generated text

Rank correlation rho = .469, p < .001

Generated text insertions are part of the writing process and can be linked to text quality.
