# Problem

Ames and Archer examine how perceived classroom achievement goals relate to students' learning strategies and motivation. The source is foundational for connecting mastery-oriented classroom climates with adaptive strategy use and motivation processes. It is useful for the wiki when modelling how feedback, assessment, and AI tasks can frame learning as improvement rather than performance display.

## Why this matters for the wiki

This source clarifies one part of the larger wiki problem: how student learning, feedback, AI support, motivation, self-beliefs, assessment, or research-method evidence should be interpreted without collapsing distinct constructs into one broad AI/education category.
