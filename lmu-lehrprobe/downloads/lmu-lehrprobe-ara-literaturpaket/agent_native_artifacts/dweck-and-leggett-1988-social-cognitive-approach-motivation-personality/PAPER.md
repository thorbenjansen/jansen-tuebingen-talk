## Summary

Foundational social-cognitive model linking implicit theories, achievement goals, and mastery-oriented versus helpless response patterns under challenge.

## Why this matters for the LMU Lehrprobe

This source helps explain why the same performance value can have different diagnostic meanings once learner characteristics, situation, feedback and process are considered.

## Key claims
- The source explains why students of similar ability can respond very differently to the same challenge or failure experience.
- Implicit theories of intelligence orient learners toward performance goals or learning goals, which then shape cognition, affect and behavior under difficulty.
- Performance goals can create vulnerability to helpless patterns when ability is in doubt, whereas learning goals support mastery-oriented responses.
- The model treats individual differences as person-situation processes: dispositions alter the probability of goals, but situational cues can also shift goals and behavior.
- For the LMU Lehrprobe, this source is a conceptual anchor for explaining why the same grade or feedback episode can mean different things for different learners.
