# Source Scope

## Strong Uses

- Learning-skills interventions are strongest when they are situated in meaningful content and tasks, rather than taught as isolated generic techniques.
- Metacognitive awareness and active learner engagement are important conditions for transfer beyond simple mnemonic performance.
- The SOLO model helps describe intervention complexity and can connect learning-process evidence to writing-process rubrics.
- The study supports a distinction between performance on trained tasks and broader learning transfer.

## Weak Or Cautious Uses

- Older meta-analysis with intervention designs and school contexts predating digital and AI-supported learning.
- Transfer categories are conceptually valuable but require caution when mapping to modern writing-with-AI settings.
