# Abstract Passage Summary

The abstract frames self-beliefs as important for students' academic success, well-being, and developmental outcomes. The review integrates 105 first-order meta-analyses on variables associated with self-beliefs during K-12 education.

Key abstract-level results:

- Personal characteristics show stronger relations with self-beliefs than interventions.
- Achievement and noncognitive variables are the strongest personal-characteristic categories.
- Interventions targeting self-beliefs directly show larger effects than interventions focused on teaching/classroom structure.
- Few meta-analyses examine situational aspects such as geographical origin.

Source location: p. 1287.
