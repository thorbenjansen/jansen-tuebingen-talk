# Introduction Passage Summary

The introduction defines self-beliefs as subjective beliefs about oneself that shape what people think and do. The authors situate self-beliefs within developmental psychology and K-12 education, arguing that childhood and adolescence are crucial periods for self-belief formation.

The paper integrates several theoretical traditions:

- social cognitive theories;
- expectancy-value theories;
- sociometer theory;
- self-concept/self-esteem/self-efficacy research.

The source uses the broad term `self-beliefs` to integrate constructs that are often studied separately.
