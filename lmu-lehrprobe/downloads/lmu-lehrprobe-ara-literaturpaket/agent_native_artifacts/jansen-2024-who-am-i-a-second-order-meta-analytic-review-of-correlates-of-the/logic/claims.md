# Claims

## C01: The paper maps the meta-analytic evidence on K-12 students' self-beliefs

- **Statement**: The review integrates 105 first-order meta-analyses on variables associated with self-beliefs in childhood and adolescence during K-12 education.
- **Status**: source-checked
- **Evidence**: E01, E02
- **Interpretation**: This is a second-order meta-analytic review, not a primary empirical study and not a single intervention trial.
- **Tags**: self-beliefs, second-order-meta-analysis, K-12, evidence-map

## C02: Personal characteristics relate more strongly to self-beliefs than interventions do

- **Statement**: Personal characteristics show a larger second-order association with self-beliefs (SMD = 0.50 [0.42, 0.59]) than interventions do (SMD = 0.27 [0.19, 0.34]).
- **Status**: source-checked
- **Evidence**: E03
- **Interpretation**: The comparison is descriptive and aggregated across first-order meta-analyses. It should not be read as showing that personal characteristics causally determine self-beliefs more than interventions can change them.
- **Tags**: personal-characteristics, interventions, self-beliefs, effect-size

## C03: Achievement and noncognitive variables are the strongest personal-characteristic correlates

- **Statement**: Among personal characteristics, achievement (SMD = 0.66 [0.57, 0.75]) and noncognitive variables (SMD = 0.67 [0.54, 0.80]) are more strongly related to self-beliefs than cognitive ability (SMD = 0.30 [0.15, 0.46]) and background variables (SMD = 0.21 [0.17, 0.25]).
- **Status**: source-checked
- **Evidence**: E04
- **Interpretation**: The result supports treating achievement experiences and socioemotional/motivational variables as central correlates of students' self-beliefs.
- **Tags**: achievement, noncognitive-variables, cognitive-ability, background-variables

## C04: Direct self-belief interventions show the strongest intervention effects

- **Statement**: Interventions targeting personal outcomes relate more strongly to self-beliefs than teaching/classroom-structure interventions, and interventions directly targeting self-beliefs show the largest intervention effect (SMD = 0.52 [0.48, 0.55]).
- **Status**: source-checked
- **Evidence**: E05
- **Interpretation**: For intervention design, the paper suggests that direct self-belief support is more effective than broad or indirect classroom-structure change when the target outcome is self-beliefs.
- **Tags**: intervention, self-belief-intervention, teaching, classroom-structure

## C05: Situational correlates are theoretically important but underrepresented in meta-analytic evidence

- **Statement**: The paper argues that situational characteristics should matter for self-beliefs, but only a small number of meta-analyses focused explicitly on such factors, preventing meaningful integrated effect-size comparisons.
- **Status**: source-checked
- **Evidence**: E06
- **Interpretation**: This is a source gap: classroom environments, cultural context, and social contexts are important but less well mapped in the meta-analytic literature than personal characteristics and interventions.
- **Tags**: situational-characteristics, research-gap, classroom-context, culture

## C06: The results are descriptive and cannot establish causal direction

- **Statement**: Because the review summarizes many correlational meta-analyses, it cannot establish causal direction for most associations between variables and self-beliefs.
- **Status**: source-checked
- **Evidence**: E07
- **Interpretation**: This caveat is critical for later wiki synthesis. Use "relates to" or "is associated with" unless discussing intervention evidence.
- **Tags**: causal-caution, correlational-evidence, interpretation-boundary

## C07: Meta-analytic transparency is improving but open-science practices remain incomplete

- **Statement**: The included meta-analyses fulfilled about 55% of transparency criteria on average; newer meta-analyses fulfilled more criteria, but preregistration, data publication, and code publication remained uncommon.
- **Status**: source-checked
- **Evidence**: E08
- **Interpretation**: The paper contributes not only substantive findings about self-beliefs but also a methodological diagnosis of the field.
- **Tags**: transparency, reproducibility, risk-of-bias, open-science
