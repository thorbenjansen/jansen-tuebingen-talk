# Methods, Variables, Measures, Outcomes

## Source Type

Second-order meta-analytic review and methodological review of first-order meta-analyses.

## Research Questions

1. What is the relative strength of relations of personal characteristics and interventions with children’s and adolescents’ self-beliefs?
2. What is the relative strength of relations of achievement, cognitive ability, noncognitive variables, and background variables with self-beliefs?
3. What is the relative strength of relations between different types of interventions and self-beliefs?
4. How do situational characteristics relate to self-beliefs?
5. How high is the risk of bias in the included meta-analyses?

## Population / Sample

- Target population: children and adolescents in K-12 education.
- Included evidence: 105 first-order meta-analyses published before July 2023.
- Scale: 493 first-order effect sizes, more than 8,500 primary studies, and more than 16 million children/adolescents.
- Inclusion boundary: meta-analyses had to report at least one effect size between a variable and a self-belief construct, include quantitative studies, focus at least 50% on K-12 children/adolescents, include general-population samples, and be available in English.

## Search and Selection

- Multimodal search strategy: database search, cross-reference checks, and cited-by checks.
- Databases included ERIC, APA PsycArticles, PSYNDEX, APA PsycBooks, APA PsycExtra, APA PsycInfo, Psychoanalytic Electronic Publishing Archive, Academic Search Complete, Health and Psychosocial Instruments, Psychology and Behavioral Sciences Collection, and Medline.
- Search completed in June 2023.
- Screening involved four raters; two raters screened every abstract.

## Coding

- Meta-analyses were double-coded.
- The coding scheme covered effect sizes, variable categorization, content-related moderators, methodological moderators, and transparency/reproducibility criteria.
- Interrater reliability averaged Cohen's kappa = .96 across coded variables.
- Disagreements were resolved by joint review of full texts.

## Variables / Categories

### Outcome

- Self-beliefs, broadly construed as enduring evaluative beliefs about the self.
- Includes constructs such as self-concept, self-esteem, self-worth, and self-efficacy.
- Includes global and domain-specific self-beliefs.

### Personal characteristics

- Achievement: language, STEM, mixed-domain achievement, persistence.
- Cognitive ability: learning disabilities and giftedness.
- Noncognitive variables: emotions, other self-beliefs, motivation, problematic technology use, perceived social support.
- Background variables: gender and ethnicity.

### Interventions

- Personal outcome interventions: direct self-belief interventions, psychological interventions, physical activity interventions.
- Teaching/classroom structure interventions: learning and teaching, school context, technology.

### Situational characteristics

- Country, birth cohort, and socioeconomic status were discussed narratively because the evidence base was too small and lacked common reference categories for integrated effect-size synthesis.

## Effect-Size Strategy

- First-order effect sizes were converted to standardized mean differences (SMD).
- Second-order meta-analyses used random-effects models.
- Robust variance estimation was used to handle dependent effect sizes from the same meta-analysis.
- Weights considered sampling error, number of studies, and primary-study overlap.

## Main Outcomes

| Comparison | SMD | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---|
| Overall second-order estimate | 0.40 [0.34, 0.46] | medium overall association |
| Personal characteristics | 0.50 [0.42, 0.59] | large |
| Interventions | 0.27 [0.19, 0.34] | medium |
| Achievement | 0.66 [0.57, 0.75] | strong personal-characteristic correlate |
| Cognitive ability | 0.30 [0.15, 0.46] | moderate |
| Noncognitive variables | 0.67 [0.54, 0.80] | strong personal-characteristic correlate |
| Background variables | 0.21 [0.17, 0.25] | small |
| Direct self-belief interventions | 0.52 [0.48, 0.55] | strongest intervention subcategory |
| Teaching/classroom structure interventions | 0.20 [0.10, 0.29] | smaller intervention category |

## Limitations / Transfer Boundaries

- Most relations are correlational; causal direction is generally not established.
- Self-beliefs were aggregated broadly across constructs and domains, which may hide domain-specific patterns.
- Shared method variance may inflate relations among self-report variables, especially noncognitive predictors.
- Some samples included mixed K-12 and non-K-12 populations to preserve evidence coverage.
- Large meta-analyses can strongly influence weighted second-order estimates.
- Moderator evidence was limited, unevenly distributed, and often incomparable across first-order meta-analyses.
- The evidence base is culturally skewed: many primary studies came from North America and Europe, and English-language studies dominate.
- Between-person associations cannot be directly translated into within-person mechanisms.
