# Problem

## Source Problem

Many studies and first-order meta-analyses examine correlates of students' self-beliefs, but the literature is fragmented across constructs, theories, domains, and variable families. Without a second-order synthesis, it is difficult to see which broad kinds of variables relate most strongly to self-beliefs and where the evidence base is thin.

## Why This Matters

Positive self-beliefs matter for students' academic success, motivation, well-being, and development. For the wiki, this paper helps explain why achievement experiences, socioemotional variables, motivation, feedback from social environments, and targeted interventions matter for student development.

## Source Gap Addressed

The paper addresses two gaps:

- It compares broad categories of correlates and interventions using second-order meta-analysis.
- It reviews the transparency and reproducibility of the underlying meta-analytic literature.

## Remaining Gap

The source does not provide causal mechanisms for most associations. It also shows that situational and cultural correlates of self-beliefs are underrepresented in meta-analytic evidence.
