# Related Work

## Theoretical Traditions Integrated

- Social cognitive theories: self-beliefs develop through interaction among personal characteristics, environment, and cognitive processes.
- Expectancy-value theories: expectancy beliefs and value beliefs shape motivation; self-beliefs relate especially to competence expectations.
- Sociometer theory: self-esteem monitors perceived relational value and social acceptance.
- Self-concept/self-esteem/self-efficacy research: the paper integrates multiple terms under the broad self-beliefs umbrella.

## Prior Meta-Analytic Work

The review integrates first-order meta-analyses on achievement, cognitive ability, noncognitive variables, background variables, interventions, technology, school context, and social support. It also relates to Jansen et al. (2022) on academic motivation by using a similar second-order meta-analytic logic for K-12 education.
