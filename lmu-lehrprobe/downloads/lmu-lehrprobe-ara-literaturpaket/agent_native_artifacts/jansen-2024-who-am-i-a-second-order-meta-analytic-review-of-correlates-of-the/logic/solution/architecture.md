# Source Architecture

## Article Structure

1. Conceptual framing of the self and self-beliefs.
2. Theoretical perspectives: social cognitive theories, expectancy-value theories, and sociometer theory.
3. Rationale for a second-order meta-analytic review.
4. Research questions.
5. Method: search, inclusion criteria, coding, effect-size conversion, and robust second-order meta-analysis.
6. Results: overall effect, personal-characteristic categories, intervention categories, situational characteristics, transparency/risk of bias.
7. Discussion: key findings, implications, limitations, and future research.

## ARA Structure

The ARA preserves that logic: `PAPER.md` gives the overview, `logic/claims.md` stores the claims, `logic/methods_variables_outcomes.md` stores design and variables, and `evidence/README.md` maps claims to source locations.
