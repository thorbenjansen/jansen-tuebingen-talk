# Constraints

- The review works at a high level of aggregation; it is not designed to answer very specific construct-domain questions without consulting the first-order meta-analyses.
- Correlational meta-analyses dominate the personal-characteristic area, so causal direction is not established.
- The review includes broad self-belief constructs and domains under one umbrella, which supports overview but limits specificity.
- Some K-12 inclusion boundaries allow mixed samples when at least 50% of participants were children/adolescents in K-12 education.
- The evidence base is culturally and linguistically skewed toward English-language and Western research.
