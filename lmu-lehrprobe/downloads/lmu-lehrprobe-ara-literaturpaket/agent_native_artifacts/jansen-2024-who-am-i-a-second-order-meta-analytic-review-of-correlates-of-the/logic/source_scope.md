# Source Scope

## Strong Uses

Use this ARA to support claims about:

- broad correlates of K-12 students' self-beliefs;
- relative strength of personal-characteristic and intervention categories;
- links among achievement, noncognitive variables, motivation, and self-beliefs;
- intervention categories that may support positive self-beliefs;
- methodological transparency in meta-analytic literature on self-beliefs.

## Weak or Unsafe Uses

Do not use this ARA to claim:

- that achievement, emotions, or motivation causally cause self-beliefs in general;
- that the same effects hold for university students or adults;
- that all self-belief constructs behave identically;
- that cultural or ethnic differences are well established;
- that specific classroom practices have strong meta-analytic evidence for self-belief outcomes.

## Citation-Grade Status

The curated ARA is citation-grade for the headline sample sizes, research questions, effect-size pattern, broad conclusions, and limitations listed in the evidence map. For exact wording or detailed moderators, verify against the raw PDF or the supplemental materials.
