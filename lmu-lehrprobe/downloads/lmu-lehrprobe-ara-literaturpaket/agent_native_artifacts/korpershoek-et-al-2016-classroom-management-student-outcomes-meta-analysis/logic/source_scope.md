# Source Scope

## Strong Uses

- Classroom management interventions show small but meaningful effects across student outcomes, with weaker evidence for motivational outcomes.
- Programs focused on students' social-emotional development contribute especially to social-emotional outcomes.
- Teacher-focused classroom management may benefit academic outcomes, making the teacher and instructional context relevant to student learning processes.
- Classroom management should be treated as a condition for productive feedback, collaboration, and AI use rather than as a separate discipline issue.

## Weak Or Cautious Uses

- Primary-education focus; transfer to secondary AI-writing settings must be indirect.
- Program components are aggregated, so mechanisms require careful interpretation.
