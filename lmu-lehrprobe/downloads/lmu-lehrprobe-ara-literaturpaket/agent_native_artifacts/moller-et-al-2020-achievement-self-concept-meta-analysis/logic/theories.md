# Theories

## Internal/external frame of reference model

- **Theory concept:** [[Internal External Frame Of Reference Model]]
- **Role in this source:** Achievement in one domain can support corresponding self-concept while affecting non-corresponding domain self-concepts through dimensional comparisons.
- **Evidence pointer:** `PAPER.md`, `logic/claims.md`, and the source PDF/Markdown.
- **APA reference:** Marsh, H. W. (1986). Verbal and math self-concepts: An internal/external frame of reference model. *American Educational Research Journal, 23*(1), 129-149.

- **Additional APA anchor:** Moeller, J., Pohlmann, B., Koeller, O., & Marsh, H. W. (2009). A meta-analytic path analysis of the internal/external frame of reference model of academic achievement and academic self-concept. *Review of Educational Research*.

## Dimensional comparison theory

- **Theory concept:** [[Dimensional Comparison Theory]]
- **Role in this source:** Students compare achievement across subjects when constructing academic self-concepts.
- **Evidence pointer:** `PAPER.md`, `logic/claims.md`, and the source PDF/Markdown.
- **APA reference:** Moeller, J., Pohlmann, B., Koeller, O., & Marsh, H. W. (2009). A meta-analytic path analysis of the internal/external frame of reference model of academic achievement and academic self-concept. *Review of Educational Research*.

- **Additional APA anchor:** Moeller, J., Zitzmann, S., Helm, F., Machts, N., & Wolff, F. (2020). A meta-analysis of relations between achievement and self-concept. *Review of Educational Research*.
