## Overview

Praetorius et al. argue that research on generic teaching quality has often worked with models rather than explicit theories of teaching. They use the Three Basic Dimensions model, built around classroom management, student support, and cognitive activation, as a focal case for asking how a widely used teaching-quality model could be developed into a stronger theory.

The paper reconstructs the model's roots in German and international teaching-quality research, reviews evidence that the three dimensions recur across studies, and then evaluates the model against criteria for a good theory of instruction. Its key contribution is not a new empirical estimate, but a theory-building agenda: clarify construct definitions, specify relations among dimensions, state boundary conditions, connect mechanisms to student outcomes, and formulate more testable propositions.
